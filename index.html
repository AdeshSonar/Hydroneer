What is pH?

pH is a measurement of the acidity or alkalinity of a solution.
It provides a value on a scale from 0 to 14 where 7 is neutral, less than 7 is acidic, and greater than 7 is alkaline (or basic).
The closer you move towards 0, the more a solution is acidic, and the closer you move to 14, the more a solution is alkaline.

ORP (Oxidation Reduction Potential)

What is ORP?

Oxidation-reduction potential or ORP is a measurement that indicates the degree to which a substance is capable of oxidizing or reducing another substance.
ORP is measured in millivolts (mV) using an ORP meter.
A positive ORP reading indicates that a substance is an oxidizing agent.
The higher the reading, the more oxidizing it is.
As such, a substance with an ORP reading of +400 mV is 4 times more oxidizing than a substance with an ORP reading of +100 mV.
A negative ORP reading indicates that a substance is a reducing agent.
The lower the reading, the more anti-oxidizing it is.
As such, a substance with an ORP reading of -400 mV is 4 times more anti-oxidizing than a substance with an ORP reading of -100 mV.

Benefits

Water with negative ORP is believed to help hydrate cells at a faster rate, help reduce inflammation and prevent cell damage caused by free radicals.
Negative ORP water helps to enhance immune system function, reducing the risk of infection and illness.

Micro-Cluster Water

What is Micro-Cluster Water?

Water molecules come in clusters rather than single molecules.
Tap water which is under pressure has very large clusters of 15-20 molecules.
The ionization process breaks the electrical bonding of water molecules and restructures the water down to about 5-6 molecules per cluster.

Benefits

Smaller cluster size means that the water can be more easily absorbed into the cells, thereby providing superior hydration for the body and helping to dissolve and flush out acidic solid waste and toxins that have accumulated in the body.
